Knife Skills 101

Master the basics of chopping, slicing, and dicing to make your time in the kitchen faster, safer, and more enjoyable.

Essential Knife Skills

1. The Claw Grip

Protect your fingers by curling them into a claw shape while holding the food. This keeps your fingertips safe while guiding the knife.

2. The Rock Chop

Use a rocking motion with your knife to chop herbs or vegetables quickly and efficiently. Keep the tip of the knife on the cutting board.

3. The Slice

For slicing, use a smooth, even motion. Keep the blade at a slight angle and let the knife do the work.

4. The Dice

To dice, first slice the food into planks, then sticks, and finally cubes. This technique works great for onions, carrots, and potatoes.

5. The Julienne

Cut food into thin, matchstick-sized strips. This is perfect for stir-fries and salads.

Tips for Mastering Knife Skills

  • Use a Sharp Knife: A sharp knife is safer and more efficient than a dull one.
  • Practice Proper Grip: Hold the knife handle firmly but not too tightly.
  • Keep Your Cutting Board Stable: Place a damp towel under the board to prevent slipping.
  • Take Your Time: Speed comes with practice. Focus on accuracy first.
